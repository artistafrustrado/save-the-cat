In order for you to utilize setting to its fullest when writing dialog, you need to anchor each scene's location as soon as possible. I weaves the setting in dialog exchanges and paint the reader firmly in the time and place of the scene.

The overall setting of the book needs to be identified at the opening, but the setting of each scene should be vividly evoked for the reader.

Three Key Points

Dwight Swann says there are three key points you need to remember about the world in which your story takes place.

  • 1 - Your reader never has been there.
  • 2 - It is a sensory world.
  • 3 - It is a subjective world.

Your reader never has been there.

The reader naver had been to this place but has gone to similar places and makes connections by association.

It is a sensory world.

We live in a world of colors, smells, textures, sound, and tastes.

What your hero or heroine sees.smells, touches, and tastes will add vivid imagery and umpact to what they say.

The five senses are also usefull in determining how the character percieves the world around them because we often favor onse sense over the other.

It's a subjective world.

We each have our own version of reality based on our life experiences.

Different aspects of the setting are going to impact each character differently. The age of the character, the gender of the character, the socioeconomica background if the characters, etc. All impact how the character is affected by the setting.
